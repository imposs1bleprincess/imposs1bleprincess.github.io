Life Flows

Use of hybrid vertical & horizontal mirroring techniques to exhibit an as above so below composite. The photo collection process for Life Flows began at sunset while foraging for inspiration within the tall chapparal that encloses my apartment. Slithering its way into my view, I made a friend in a garden snake as I snapped photos of the horizon.

Hyphaeic

Her nerves spread like wings at mycelium speed. Into the atmosphere, spores everywhere
- Bjork

Inspired by the ecological soundscapes of Bjork's Fossora, I grew an addiction for the early morning silence. Hyphaeic is an artwork embodying the ability to listen to nature's quiet and lush whispers. The undoctored image was captured on UCSB near Storke Tower & uses mirroring & dynamic blending techniques.

Distant Relations

Geography taught me about Tobler's Law-- that with distance, objects become less related to each other. With this project, I wanted to exhibit an artistic representation of the inverse of what Tobler's Law states.

Elements of Life

More photo-mirroring & extensive photo blending techniques with the intention of creating or more so unveiling alien-like plant life. I wanted to explore the possibilities of what plant life could look like on other planets while upholding its familiar presence.

Biodata

This is a display of technology used in a biological data collection setup which incorporates an EEG and a hear-rate monitor that are connected to a lighting system. This setup was a component of a larger audio-visual project which used the biodata as a modulator for both audio & visuals created for the exhibit.
